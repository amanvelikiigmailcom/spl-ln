## Why people are leaving Otter.ai in 2026 {#why-leaving}

Users are leaving Otter.ai for four connected reasons: a damaging privacy lawsuit, bot fatigue, language limitations, and a free tier that's become nearly unusable.

The most significant development happened in August 2025, when a class action lawsuit (Brewer v. Otter.ai) was filed claiming that Otter's AI transcription service secretly recorded private conversations without participant consent. According to NPR's reporting on the case, Otter's OtterPilot feature "does not by default ask all the other participants" for permission to record and "fails to alert participants that recordings are shared with Otter to improve its artificial intelligence systems." Three follow-on lawsuits (Walker, Theus, and Winston) were filed within a month. Legal claims span the federal Electronic Communications Privacy Act, California's Invasion of Privacy Act, and Illinois's Biometric Information Privacy Act.

That's the legal side. But even before the lawsuit, practitioners in regulated fields were already uncomfortable. If you work in law, medicine, or HR, the moment a client sees "OtterPilot has joined the meeting" in the participant list, trust breaks. I've spoken with legal ops professionals who said this single notification cost them client confidence in high-stakes consultations. It's not paranoia. It's professional risk.

The language situation is also a real constraint. Otter supports only four languages: English, French, Japanese, and Spanish. Most competitors now handle 50 to 140+ languages. If you work with international clients or multilingual teams, Otter simply stops working.

And the free plan, which used to be generous, now caps you at 300 minutes per month with a 30-minute-per-conversation limit. For daily professional use, that's not workable.

## What to look for in an Otter AI alternative {#what-to-look-for}

The right Otter AI alternative depends on which of Otter's problems you're trying to solve. Here are the four criteria that matter most.

**Privacy and data handling.** This is the question post-lawsuit: where does your audio go? Cloud tools (Otter, Fireflies, Fathom, tl;dv) process audio on their servers. That means your meetings, including anything confidential, travel outside your machine. On-device tools (Siplinx AI, some versions of Whisper-based apps) process locally. Nothing leaves the computer. For anything involving client confidentiality, patient data, or sensitive HR conversations, local processing is the only defensible choice.

**Bot presence.** Does the tool require a bot to join your call? Otter's OtterPilot does. So do Fireflies, MeetGeek, and Read AI. Tools like Tactiq, Siplinx AI, Fathom (for host-side recording), and Granola work without sending a bot. No notification. No participant list disruption.

**Language support.** If you only work in English, this doesn't matter. But if you need French, German, Portuguese, or any of 50+ other languages, Otter fails immediately. Notta supports 58 languages. Fireflies supports 69+. HappyScribe handles 140+.

**Pricing and free tier.** Otter's Pro plan is $16.99 per month. Fathom has a genuinely unlimited free plan (for Zoom users). Tactiq's free tier handles 10 meetings per month. Siplinx AI's pricing is available at siplinx.com. Before paying for anything, check whether the free tier is actually usable for your volume.

## Best Otter AI alternative for privacy and offline use {#best-for-privacy}

For users who need full privacy and offline capability, [Siplinx AI is the clearest answer](https://siplinx.com/features/?utm_source=own_blog&utm_medium=article&utm_campaign=otter-ai-alternative&utm_content=privacy-section) in this category.

![A desktop computer showing local AI processing with no cloud connectivity icons](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1558494949-ef010cbdcc31?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80)

Siplinx AI is a desktop application for Mac and Windows that transcribes, summarizes, and extracts action items from meetings entirely on-device. It uses a local speech-to-text engine and a local LLM. No audio, transcripts, or summaries are sent to any server. Ever. This isn't a privacy policy promise. It's a technical fact: the network connection plays no role in the processing pipeline.

This matters specifically if you're a lawyer, doctor, or consultant. The concerns that drove the Otter.ai lawsuit (secret recording, data used for AI training, participants unaware their words were being stored on someone else's servers) are impossible with Siplinx by design. There's no server to store anything on.

The other tools worth knowing in this tier:

**Meetily** (open source) runs entirely locally on your machine and supports pluggable AI summaries. It's free and built for privacy-first users who are comfortable with a technical setup. No meeting bot.

**Jamie** works offline for in-person meetings (no bot, no cloud during recording) though it does use cloud for AI summaries by default. It supports 100+ languages. Pricing starts at €0 for 10 credits per month and €24 per month for the Standard plan.

**meetergo Log** uses local AI processing as its core feature and positions itself as GDPR-compliant by design. It combines scheduling and transcription in one tool.

I've tested Siplinx alongside Granola and Otter, and the thing that stood out immediately is how fast the local transcription runs. You don't notice any lag compared to cloud tools. The tradeoff is obvious: you need a reasonably capable Mac or Windows machine. But for professionals handling sensitive conversations, that's a fair trade.

## Best free Otter AI alternatives {#best-free}

Several tools offer genuinely useful free tiers. These are the ones worth knowing.

**Fathom** is the strongest free option for Zoom users. Its free plan includes unlimited recording and AI summaries with no caps. The catch: it works only with Zoom (Google Meet and Microsoft Teams require a paid plan). A bot joins the call, so participants will see it. But the quality of the summaries on the free tier is genuinely good.

**Tactiq** is a browser extension that captures transcripts in real time from Google Meet, Zoom, and Microsoft Teams without sending a bot into the meeting. The free plan handles 10 meetings per month. It doesn't store audio, which helps with privacy. The summaries are shorter than Fathom's but the absence of a bot is a meaningful advantage.

**Notta** has a free tier that gives you 120 minutes per month of transcription in 58 languages. If you primarily need multilingual support and don't need unlimited minutes, Notta's free plan is more capable than Otter's.

**Fireflies.ai** has a free plan too, but it caps storage at 800 minutes and transcription quality on the free tier is noticeably lower. Better for occasional use than regular professional work.

One thing to note: Otter's free tier is not entirely unusable at 300 minutes per month. If you have fewer than 10 short meetings per month, it may still cover you. The problem is that Otter's free tier shrank significantly from its original generosity, and the privacy concerns haven't changed.

## Best Otter AI alternatives for teams {#best-for-teams}

For teams who need shared meeting archives, collaborative notes, and integrations with project management tools, these are the leading options.

**Fireflies.ai** is used by over 200,000 organizations and integrates with 69+ languages, Slack, Notion, Salesforce, and HubSpot. Pricing starts at $18 per user per month for the Pro plan. The main downside: accuracy drops in noisy environments, and the bot presence can feel aggressive (it joins every calendar invite by default).

**MeetGeek** has 7,000+ app integrations and strong team collaboration features including customizable meeting templates and conversation analytics across 100+ KPIs. Pricing starts at $19 per user per month for Pro. It's GDPR compliant and handles multiple meeting types well.

**Fellow** is the most integration-heavy option in this tier, with features spanning scheduling, collaborative note-taking, and deep workflow tools. It's the strongest for engineering and product teams that already live in tools like Linear, Jira, or Notion. Pricing starts around $7 per user per month billed annually.

**tl;dv** has a clean interface and genuine AI coaching features (scorecards, playbooks) that make it useful for sales teams. The free plan archives for only 3 days, which is a meaningful limitation for reviews and follow-ups.

For most mid-size teams, I'd narrow the choice to Fireflies for language variety or MeetGeek for integration depth. Both are better than Otter for teams, mainly because Otter's collaboration features haven't kept pace with competitors.

## Best Otter AI alternative for regulated industries {#regulated-industries}

Lawyers, doctors, and HR professionals have specific needs that most "best alternatives" lists don't address.

The core requirement is simple: your meeting notes tool cannot be a liability. If a client's confidential conversation gets recorded on a third-party server, you have a problem regardless of what the privacy policy says. Post-Brewer lawsuit, this is no longer a theoretical concern.

For regulated professionals, the options break down like this:

**Siplinx AI** is the only tool in this list that processes everything locally with no cloud component in the transcription or summarization pipeline. For attorneys handling privileged communications or doctors dealing with patient conversations, this is the strongest technical guarantee available.

**Meetily** is the open-source alternative in the same category. It's free and fully local. The setup requires more comfort with software configuration. For a solo practitioner with time to set it up, it's a serious option.

**MeetGeek** claims GDPR compliance and does give teams control over data retention settings. But it's still cloud-based. For HIPAA-covered entities, cloud-based tools require a Business Associate Agreement (BAA). Not all providers offer this.

**Avoma** and **Gong.io** are enterprise-grade tools used heavily in sales that offer data residency options for large contracts. Gong.io starts at $5,000 per year (platform fee) plus per-user costs. These are not tools for small teams or individuals.

The practical answer for most lawyers and doctors: use a tool that doesn't touch a cloud server. The premium for local processing is worth it given what's now been demonstrated in the Otter lawsuit.

## How to switch from Otter AI in 5 steps {#how-to-switch}

Switching is straightforward. Here's the process from exporting your data to your first meeting on the new tool.

1. **Export your Otter data.** Log into otter.ai and go to Settings. Under "My Stuff," select Export. Otter lets you export transcripts as text files or PDFs. Download everything before you cancel.

2. **Pick your replacement based on your primary need.** Privacy and offline: Siplinx AI or Meetily. Free and bot-free: Tactiq. Team collaboration: Fireflies or MeetGeek. Multilingual: Notta or HappyScribe.

3. **Install and configure the new tool.** For desktop apps (Siplinx AI), run the installer and configure your audio input source. For browser extensions (Tactiq), install from the Chrome Web Store and connect your calendar. For bots (Fireflies, MeetGeek), authorize the calendar integration and configure which meetings to auto-join.

4. **Run one test meeting before going live.** Record a short internal call, check the transcript quality, and make sure action items are captured correctly. Don't switch cold for a client-facing meeting.

5. **Cancel Otter.** Go to Settings in Otter, select Subscription, and cancel before your next billing date. Note that Otter keeps deleted conversations in Trash for 30 days before permanent deletion.

The whole process takes about 20 minutes for most users. The hardest part is choosing. The switching itself is fast.

## FAQ {#faq}

**What is the best free alternative to Otter AI?**

Fathom is the best free Otter AI alternative for Zoom users. It offers unlimited recording and AI summaries at no cost on its free plan. Tactiq is the best free option if you need a bot-free approach (it works as a browser extension and handles Google Meet, Zoom, and Teams). Notta's free tier is the best if you need multilingual support (120 minutes per month, 58 languages).

**Is there an Otter AI alternative that works offline?**

Yes. Siplinx AI is a desktop application for Mac and Windows that processes everything on-device with no internet connection required for transcription or summarization. Jamie also records offline for in-person meetings, though its AI summaries use the cloud by default. Meetily (open source) is fully local as well, but requires more technical setup.

**What is better than Otter AI for privacy?**

Any tool that processes audio locally rather than sending it to a cloud server is better than Otter AI for privacy. Siplinx AI processes everything on-device. Meetily is fully open source and local. Tactiq doesn't store audio at all (it only captures the text stream). For cloud tools, Fireflies claims it doesn't train on customer data, but that claim exists in a policy document rather than in the technical architecture.

**Does Otter AI record conversations without permission?**

This is exactly what the Brewer v. Otter.ai lawsuit (filed August 2025) alleges. According to NPR's coverage of the case, Otter's OtterPilot feature does not by default ask all meeting participants for permission to record and does not notify them that recordings may be used to train Otter's AI models. Otter disputes these claims, but the lawsuit is ongoing as of mid-2026.

**What happened with the Otter AI lawsuit?**

Multiple class action lawsuits were filed between August and September 2025, starting with Brewer v. Otter.ai on August 15, 2025. The complaints allege violation of the Electronic Communications Privacy Act, California's Invasion of Privacy Act, Illinois's Biometric Information Privacy Act, the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act, and several state common-law theories. The lawsuits are ongoing as of the date of this article.

**Can I use an Otter AI alternative without a meeting bot?**

Yes. Several tools work without a bot: Tactiq (browser extension), Siplinx AI (desktop app that records system audio), Jamie (native recorder, no bot), Granola (Mac app, no bot), and meetergo Log (local AI, no bot). Tools that require a bot include Fireflies, MeetGeek, Read AI, tl;dv, and Otter's OtterPilot.

**Which Otter AI alternative works for HIPAA-compliant meetings?**

The safest technical approach for HIPAA is a tool that processes audio locally and never transmits patient conversation data to an external server. Siplinx AI fits this requirement. For cloud-based tools, HIPAA compliance requires a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with the vendor. Fireflies and MeetGeek offer BAAs for enterprise plans. Always confirm current BAA availability directly with the vendor before relying on any cloud tool for protected health information.

Otter.ai built a useful product, but 2026 is a different environment than 2018 when it launched. The class action lawsuit has made privacy in AI meeting tools a professional liability question for lawyers, doctors, and executives. The 4-language cap makes Otter a poor choice for global teams. And the free tier no longer delivers what it once did.

The good news is the market has caught up. If you need offline, private transcription for sensitive conversations, [try Siplinx AI](https://siplinx.com/?utm_source=own_blog&utm_medium=article&utm_campaign=otter-ai-alternative&utm_content=conclusion-cta) and see what it feels like to have your notes processed entirely on your own machine. If free cloud tools work for your use case, Fathom and Tactiq are both better fits than Otter for most users.

Pick the tool that matches your actual threat model. For most professionals in 2026, that means thinking harder about where your audio actually goes.
