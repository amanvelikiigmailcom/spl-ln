Informational. The user wants practical, step-by-step guidance on how to take better meeting notes — likely because their current approach loses key decisions or leaves action items untracked.

## Real user pain points (from Reddit/Quora/forums)

1. "I can't write fast enough to capture everything — I miss things while scribbling"
2. "My notes make sense during the meeting but look like gibberish the next morning"
3. "I end up transcribing everything instead of actually listening to the discussion"
4. "Action items get lost — nobody knows who is responsible for what after the meeting"
5. "I'm the designated note-taker AND the meeting host — I can't do both well at the same time"
6. "Our notes sit in a shared doc nobody reads again after the meeting ends"
7. "Remote meetings are harder — I can't see body language and people talk over each other"

These should become H2 questions and FAQ entries.

## Content gaps (what top results miss)

1. https://asana.com/resources/meeting-notes-tips — misses privacy/confidentiality concerns for notes shared in the cloud; no discussion of offline or local-first note-taking tools
2. https://slack.com/blog/productivity/how-to-take-effective-meeting-notes — heavily promotional for Slack, limited evidence for statistics cited, no guidance on handling sensitive meetings
3. https://www.performyard.com/articles/how-to-take-meeting-notes — 3,500 words but no coverage of privacy/security risks of cloud note-taking, no real-world before/after examples, assumes digital tool access

**Our angle:** All top competitors ignore the privacy dimension of meeting notes entirely. For lawyers, doctors, consultants, and executives handling confidential conversations, the question isn't just "how do I take better notes" — it's "how do I take notes without leaking sensitive information to cloud servers?" Siplinx AI processes everything locally with no data leaving the device. This is the angle no competitor touches.

## Recommended article structure

Based on research, these H2 sections will perform best:

1. What are meeting notes (and why they're different from meeting minutes)?
2. What should you always include in meeting notes?
3. How do you take meeting notes without missing what's being said?
4. What note-taking method works best for different meetings?
5. How do you turn meeting notes into action items that actually get done?
6. Should you use AI to take your meeting notes?
7. What about privacy — who can see your meeting notes?
8. FAQ
