## Real user pain points (from Reddit/forums/lawsuits/expert sources)

1. **"Otter joined a Zoom meeting with investors and shared a transcription with me that included intimate, confidential details about a business discussed after I had left."** — Direct user complaint surfaced by NPR investigation, 2025.

2. **Non-consenting participants get recorded.** Fireflies.ai auto-joins calls and records everyone in the meeting, including people who never signed up for the service and never agreed to its terms. This is now the subject of a class-action lawsuit.

3. **Meeting data is used to train AI models without explicit opt-in.** Otter.ai's lawsuit specifically alleges this. Users want transcription, not to donate their confidential calls to a training dataset.

4. **Accuracy drops badly in noisy environments.** Krisp's benchmark shows WER jumps from ~7% in clean audio to 24.1% in noisy conditions for some tools — meaning roughly 1 in 4 words is wrong.

5. **"The bot announces itself in every call."** Users find that cloud-based bots that join meetings create awkward social situations, especially with clients or in sensitive discussions.

6. **Pricing feels hidden.** Most tools advertise free tiers but hit paywalls for the features that actually matter (storage limits, custom summaries, integrations).

7. **No HIPAA-compliant option that works offline.** Healthcare and legal professionals frequently note that cloud-based tools cannot be used for patient discussions or attorney-client privileged conversations.

These should become H2 questions and FAQ entries.
