## LSI / related keywords

## Target audience

Sales reps, account executives, and sales managers who spend too much time on after-call admin work and want to capture better notes without breaking their flow during calls.

## Search intent

Informational / Commercial
Users searching "sales call notes" want to understand how to take better notes on sales calls, find templates or tools to do it faster, and potentially find AI solutions that automate the process.

## Real user pain points (from Reddit/Quora/forums)

1. "I spend more time writing up notes after the call than the call itself lasted — there has to be a better way."
2. "My notes are inconsistent across different reps; the next AE picking up the account has no idea what was discussed."
3. "We lose deal context when notes aren't synced to the CRM before the next touchpoint."
4. "I can't stay engaged in a conversation AND type notes at the same time — one always suffers."
5. "Managers want structured notes in a specific format but there's no template enforced, so everyone does it differently."

These should become H2 questions and FAQ entries.

## Content gaps (what top results miss)

1. https://www.read.ai/articles/how-to-take-and-use-sales-call-notes — covers surface-level tips but misses the privacy and data security angle when call recordings go to the cloud.
2. https://www.revenue.io/blog/call-notes-transform-your-sales-team — strong on templates but misses the specific workflow for teams who handle sensitive client information and can't use cloud recorders.
3. https://krisp.ai/blog/blog-best-ai-note-taker-for-sales-calls/ — only covers cloud-based AI tools; misses the offline/on-device alternative.

**Our angle:** Most guides assume cloud recording is fine. The Siplinx article will address the specific use case of sales teams who handle confidential conversations (finance, legal, medical sales) where cloud uploading is a compliance risk, and show how on-device AI solves this.
