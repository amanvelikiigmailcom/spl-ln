## Real user pain points (from Reddit/Quora/forums)

1. "I spend 3-4 hours a day in meetings and can never remember what was decided or who owns what action item." (common complaint pattern across productivity forums)
2. "Half the people in our meetings are just there to look busy — but I have to be there too so I can't say no." (meeting culture problem)
3. "I multitask during meetings because they're so unproductive, but then I miss things and look bad." (52% of employees multitask during virtual meetings per Microsoft data)
4. "We have pre-meetings to prepare for the actual meeting. Something is wrong." (Fortune, remote work article, Oct 2025)
5. "Every recurring meeting eventually becomes useless but no one cancels them — too political." (Instagram, Block, Shopify all addressed this at the company level)
6. "AI notetakers feel like surveillance. My boss wants us to use one but people are uncomfortable." (common privacy concern in hybrid workplaces)
7. "I get meeting summaries from AI tools but they're generic and miss the real context." (gap in current AI tools)

These should become H2 questions and FAQ entries.

2. https://www.uctoday.com/unified-communications/ai-in-meetings-the-driving-force-behind-productivity-and-collaboration/ — Strong on enterprise stats but doesn't give practical step-by-step guidance for individuals
3. https://www.techtarget.com/searchunifiedcommunications/tip/AI-meeting-assistants-to-consider — Reviews tools but ignores the "AI as surveillance" concern and doesn't compare privacy-first options

**Our angle:** Most articles focus on AI meeting features as a checklist. Our article argues that meeting productivity requires both AI tools AND meeting design changes — and that for professionals with confidential conversations (lawyers, doctors, executives), the choice of AI tool matters as much as how you use it. We position Siplinx AI as the solution for privacy-sensitive contexts.
