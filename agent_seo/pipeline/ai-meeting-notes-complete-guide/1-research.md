## Target audience

Privacy-conscious professionals (lawyers, doctors, executives, consultants) and knowledge workers who want to stop wasting time on manual note-taking and need accurate, automated meeting documentation.

## Search intent

Informational / Commercial Investigation
The user wants to understand what AI meeting notes tools exist, how they work, which is best for their use case, and whether they should trust them with their meeting data. Many are in the evaluation/comparison phase before buying.

## Real user pain points (from Reddit/Quora/forums + privacy reports)

1. "I don't want a bot joining my calls — my clients find it creepy and unprofessional"
2. "I'm a lawyer and I can't send confidential client audio to a cloud server — but every tool seems to require this"
3. "The AI summary missed half the action items and I didn't realize until the next meeting"
4. "Otter.ai transcribed someone who didn't even consent to being recorded — I got in trouble with HR"
5. "I tried three tools and they all send my data to US servers — GDPR nightmare for my EU clients"
6. "My company IT banned Fireflies because of the data residency issue"
7. "The free tiers are fine until your meeting goes over 40 minutes and then you're locked out mid-conversation"

These should become H2 questions and FAQ entries.

## Content gaps (what top results miss)

1. https://zapier.com/blog/best-ai-meeting-assistant/ — no FAQ section, no TL;DR, no real statistics cited, doesn't discuss privacy/compliance deeply, no cost-benefit analysis
2. https://www.meetjamie.ai/blog/ai-note-taker — focused on free tools only, no discussion of offline/local options, no privacy risk analysis, no GDPR/HIPAA angle
3. Otter.ai's own blog — understandably doesn't discuss the 2025 lawsuit or data training concerns

**Our angle:** Write the guide that specifically addresses the privacy gap. Most "best AI meeting notes" articles are written by or for the cloud-based tools. Siplinx AI's angle: explain why 73% of businesses haven't adopted AI meeting notes yet (privacy), what the real risks are with cloud tools (lawsuit, GDPR), and how offline tools solve this — with Siplinx as the natural example.
