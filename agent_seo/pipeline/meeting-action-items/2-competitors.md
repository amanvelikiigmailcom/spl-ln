## Competitive strategy for Siplinx article

**Target word count:** 3,832 words (beats longest competitor — fellow.ai at ~4,500 by covering the offline/privacy angle they all miss)

**H2 structure we will use** (more comprehensive than all 3):

1. What are meeting action items and why do most of them fail? (answers gap: root cause analysis all 3 skip)
2. What makes a meeting action item actually stick? (answers the "4 W's" better than Asana with depth)
3. How do you extract action items from a meeting without losing any? (critical how-to step all 3 skip or skim)
4. Can AI automatically extract action items from meetings? (timely, 2026-relevant angle)
5. How does offline AI handle action item extraction for confidential meetings? (unique angle no competitor covers)
6. What is the best system for tracking action items after a meeting? (practical workflow, RingCentral skim this)
7. How to write action items that people actually complete? (common mistakes angle, Asana covers briefly)
8. FAQ

**Our #1 differentiator:** The extraction step — how to actually pull action items out of a 60-minute conversation, especially using AI that runs locally on your device without sending audio to the cloud. All 3 competitors skip this step or treat it as solved.

**Data competitors don't have:** 54% of employees want post-meeting summaries but only 39% receive them (Claryti, 2026). Teams using automated tracking complete 91% vs. 61% with manual entry. Offline AI angle (GDPR/HIPAA) completely absent from all competitors.

**Siplinx AI angle:** Position Siplinx as the tool for professionals who need AI action item extraction but can't send meeting audio to the cloud — lawyers, doctors, executives, consultants. Frame it as: "you can have AI extraction and privacy, you don't have to choose."
