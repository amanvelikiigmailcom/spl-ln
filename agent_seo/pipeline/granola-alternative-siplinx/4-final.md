## Why people look for a Granola alternative in 2026 {#why-people-look}

Granola is a well-designed product that grew fast. By late 2025, it had surpassed one million users and raised $125 million in a Series C round at a $1.5 billion valuation. That growth is real. The tool works, and the interface is genuinely cleaner than most competitors.

But growth brings scrutiny. Three specific friction points push users toward alternatives.

**Data training by default.** Granola uses your meeting transcripts to train and improve its AI models. On the Basic and Business plans, this is automatic. You can only opt out if you're on the Enterprise plan. For users whose meetings contain anything confidential, this is a problem the pricing tier does not fully solve.

**The cost model at team scale.** Granola's Business plan runs $14 per user per month. An Enterprise seat is $35. For a 10-person team, that's $140 to $350 monthly before factoring in Zoom, Google Workspace, and every other SaaS tool the team runs. Several users have reported switching not because Granola is bad, but because the total SaaS stack became hard to justify.

**Privacy incidents that changed the conversation.** In 2025, a third-party key accidentally exposed in Granola's iOS beta made 333 user transcripts potentially accessible. Separately, a 2025 investigation found that Granola's "private by default" positioning was misleading: shared notes were viewable by anyone with a link. Neither incident destroyed Granola's reputation, but they shifted how privacy-conscious users think about the tool.

To be fair: Granola now works on Windows (launched January 2026), earned SOC 2 Type 2 certification in July 2025, and has addressed some of these concerns. But the fundamental model (audio captured locally, then sent to cloud for processing and summarization) hasn't changed.

## What Granola actually does with your meeting audio {#what-granola-does}

Granola's architecture is "bot-free," meaning no separate bot joins your call as a visible participant. Instead, it captures system audio directly from your computer. This is better than bot-based tools for user experience. It does not mean your audio stays local.

Once captured, your audio goes to Granola's servers for transcription and AI summarization. The transcript is generated in the cloud, the summary is generated in the cloud, and (unless you're on an Enterprise plan) that content contributes to model training.

The SOC 2 Type 2 certification confirms that Granola meets cloud security standards for how data is handled on their servers. It does not mean your data never reaches their servers. These are different things, and the distinction matters for regulated industries.

For a lawyer handling privileged client communications, the question isn't "is this vendor's cloud secure?" It's "should this audio exist on a vendor's cloud at all?" HIPAA analysis for AI tools in 2026 makes the same point: ambient AI meeting tools "continuously capture audio, transmit it to cloud servers for processing, and may use the data to train models, creating PHI vulnerabilities that static data entry never had."

That's the real argument for a Granola alternative. Not that Granola's cloud is insecure. It's that some data shouldn't go to any cloud.

## What to look for in a Granola alternative {#what-to-look-for}

Before comparing tools, settle on four questions.

**Where does processing happen?** Cloud tools (Granola, Fireflies, Otter, Fathom) send audio to their servers. Local tools (Siplinx AI, OpenWhispr) run everything on your device. If your data is regulated or sensitive, local processing removes a full category of risk.

**Which platforms does it support?** Granola added Windows in January 2026, but the Windows version is newer with fewer users testing it than the Mac version. If cross-platform reliability matters, confirm Windows support is fully stable.

**Does it work without internet?** Most cloud tools fail silently when connectivity drops. A local tool works in a conference room with bad Wi-Fi, on a plane, or in a secure facility with restricted network access.

**What's the real long-term cost?** Per-seat SaaS subscriptions compound. A tool with a one-time license or flat team pricing may cost significantly less over 24 months, even if the upfront number looks higher.

## The best Granola alternatives compared {#best-alternatives}

![Comparison of AI meeting note-taker interfaces on Mac and Windows screens](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1531297484001-80022131f5a1?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80)

### Siplinx AI: best for professionals who can't use the cloud

Siplinx AI is a desktop application for Mac and Windows that runs speech-to-text and AI summarization entirely on your device. No audio is transmitted anywhere. No internet connection is needed. The local LLM generates your summaries without touching a cloud API.

This is the only Granola alternative on this list that is genuinely offline by design. Not "privacy-first" as a marketing phrase, but structurally incapable of sending data out because no network calls are made during recording or processing.

It works with Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, and in-person meetings. It captures system audio without joining calls as a bot. Speaker identification runs locally. Summaries, action items, and formatted notes are all generated on-device.

The use case is specific: lawyers, doctors, executives, consultants, and researchers who operate in environments where cloud transmission of meeting content is legally or ethically ruled out. [Siplinx AI's approach to local processing](https://siplinx.com/?utm_source=own_blog&utm_medium=article&utm_campaign=granola-alternative-siplinx&utm_content=local-processing-intro) means your meeting content never becomes someone else's training data.

One honest note: Siplinx AI doesn't have Granola's consumer-polished interface. Granola's UI is cleaner, and I think that matters for daily use if you're not operating under data restrictions. But if the question is "where does my audio go?", Siplinx AI has the only unambiguous answer.

### Fireflies.ai: best for sales teams with CRM needs

Fireflies is a cloud-based meeting recorder with extensive integrations into Salesforce, HubSpot, Notion, Slack, and 40+ other tools. A bot joins your calls and captures everything searchably.

Good for: high-volume sales teams who need meeting context fed directly into CRM records.

Not good for: anyone with data that requires privacy controls. All audio and transcripts live on Fireflies' servers.

### Otter.ai: best for live captions and accessibility

Otter has been in the transcription space since 2016. Its real-time caption feature is genuinely useful for accessibility. The free tier allows 300 minutes per month.

Good for: individual users who primarily need live captions, or very low-volume meeting notes.

Not good for: privacy-sensitive use cases. All processing is cloud-based. The UI has accumulated clutter over the years from repeated product pivots.

### Fathom: best free option for individual Zoom users

Fathom has a genuinely unlimited free tier for individuals. It launched bot-free recording in October 2025, which puts it closer to Granola's architecture. Summaries are fast.

Good for: individual users on Zoom who have no sensitive data and want zero cost to start.

Not good for: teams (team features require paid plans), users on platforms other than Zoom and Google Meet, or anyone with regulated data.

### Jamie: best for local-ish recording with structured output

Jamie records locally (without a bot) and processes audio on its own servers after the meeting. It claims not to store audio after processing. It produces well-structured summaries with clear action items and supports 100+ languages.

Good for: users who want better-structured output than Granola's freeform summaries, and who are comfortable with cloud processing if audio isn't retained.

Not good for: true offline use, or environments where even temporary cloud transmission is prohibited.

## Who should switch from Granola {#who-should-switch}

**Switch to Siplinx AI if:**

- Your meetings involve client conversations covered by attorney-client privilege
- You handle patient information, clinical discussions, or any PHI
- Your organization operates in finance, government, or defense with data residency requirements
- You work in locations without reliable internet (travel, secure facilities, remote offices)
- You want your team on Mac and Windows without feature disparity
- You're evaluating long-term cost and want to avoid per-seat subscription growth

**Stay with Granola if:**

- Your meetings contain no regulated or confidential content
- You value Granola's interface and are comfortable with its privacy terms
- Cloud processing is acceptable for your use case
- You're on the Enterprise plan and have opted out of model training

**Switch to Fireflies** if you run a sales team and meeting-to-CRM integration is the priority.

**Switch to Fathom** if you're an individual on Zoom and want the free tier.

**Switch to Jamie** if you want structured summaries and are comfortable with post-meeting cloud processing.

## FAQ {#faq}

**What is the best free alternative to Granola AI?**

Fathom is the best free option for individual users on Zoom, with no meeting limit on its free tier. Otter.ai offers 300 free minutes per month. Both are cloud-based. For a free offline option, OpenWhispr is open-source and runs locally, though it requires more technical setup than Siplinx AI or Fathom.

**Does Granola AI work on Windows?**

Yes, as of January 2026. Granola added Windows support after years as a Mac-only app. The Windows version is newer, so feature parity with the Mac version is still catching up. Siplinx AI has offered native Windows support since launch.

**Is Granola AI HIPAA compliant?**

Granola has SOC 2 Type 2 certification (achieved July 2025), but SOC 2 is not the same as HIPAA compliance. For HIPAA compliance, you need a signed Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with the vendor. Granola does not publicly offer a BAA on its standard plans. Healthcare teams with PHI in meetings should verify this directly with Granola's sales team, or use a tool like Siplinx AI where audio never leaves the device, removing the BAA question entirely.

**What happens to my meeting audio in Granola?**

Granola captures system audio locally, then sends it to cloud servers for transcription and AI summarization. On Basic and Business plans, that content is used to improve Granola's AI models by default. Enterprise plan users can opt out. In 2025, an iOS beta incident exposed 333 user transcripts due to a leaked third-party key.

**Can I use an AI meeting notes app without internet access?**

Yes, with Siplinx AI. It runs local speech-to-text (Whisper-based) and a local LLM entirely on your device. You can record, transcribe, and summarize meetings with no internet connection. Most other tools on this list require a live connection to function.

**How does Siplinx AI compare to Granola on transcription accuracy?**

Both use Whisper-class speech recognition. Accuracy is comparable in standard English for one-to-two person calls. For heavy accents, highly technical vocabulary, or meetings with five or more speakers, results vary by model configuration. Siplinx AI lets you choose which local Whisper model to run, giving you control over the accuracy-speed tradeoff. Granola doesn't offer that choice because processing happens server-side.

**What AI meeting notes tool is best for lawyers and healthcare teams?**

Siplinx AI. It processes everything on-device with no data transmission, which means no third-party cloud exposure, no risk of a vendor breach affecting your meeting content, and no BAA requirement. For situations where cloud transmission is legally prohibited or ethically problematic, local processing is the only viable answer.
