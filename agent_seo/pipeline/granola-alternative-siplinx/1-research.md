## Real user pain points (from Reddit/forums/reviews)

1. **"I love the interface and the way it handles privacy, but I really need a Windows version for my work laptop."** — User frustration with Mac-only limitation (Granola added Windows in Jan 2026, but still limited)
2. **Granola's notes are public by default** — the app claims "private by default" but shared links are viewable by anyone; default settings expose sensitive discussions
3. **No audio/video playback** — Granola does not record audio, so if speaker identification is wrong or a transcript is garbled, there is no way to verify or correct it
4. **Data used for AI model training by default** — only Enterprise plan users can opt out of model training; all other tiers contribute transcripts to Granola's training data
5. **Subscription cost scales fast for teams** — $14/user/month (Business) or $35/user/month (Enterprise); 10-seat team = $140-$350/month on top of other SaaS costs
6. **The 25-meeting lifetime free tier isn't enough to evaluate seriously** — multiple Reddit users mention hitting the limit quickly
7. **Mixed accuracy on technical vocabulary and numbers** — users report "messed up numbers" and speaker confusion in multi-person calls

**Our angle:** Every major competitor article recommends cloud alternatives to a cloud tool. None of them address the fundamental question: what if you need your audio to never leave your device at all? The Siplinx article owns the "truly offline, local AI" angle — and can cite real privacy incidents (Cluely breach, Samsung ChatGPT incident, Granola's own data training default) to make the case.
