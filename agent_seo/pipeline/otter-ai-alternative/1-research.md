## Real user pain points (from Reddit/Quora/forums)

1. "Otter was secretly recording a confidential conversation" — The Brewer lawsuit reflects what many users feared: the OtterPilot bot joins calls without explicit participant consent, recording people who never agreed to it.

2. "Clients and partners dread the 'OtterPilot has joined the meeting' notification" — Bot fatigue is real. In legal consultations or HR meetings, a visible bot breaks trust immediately.

3. "Otter only supports English, French, Japanese, and Spanish" — Global teams dealing with accented speakers or other languages find accuracy drops dramatically.

4. "I found transcripts of conversations I never knew were being recorded" — Reddit users reported finding unexpected email summaries of meetings they participated in without an Otter account themselves.

5. "The free plan got more restrictive over time — 30 minutes monthly is not usable" — Otter's free tier limits frustrate users who want a real alternative with more generous free access.

6. "Otter keeps deleted conversations in Trash for 30 days and processes everything in the cloud" — Privacy-sensitive users in healthcare and legal find this unacceptable.

These become H2 questions and FAQ entries.
