## Real user pain points (from Reddit/Quora/forums)

1. Google Meet's built-in Gemini notes require Business Standard or higher (starts at $14/user/month) — the free tier gets nothing.
2. The bot-in-meeting problem: most third-party AI note takers join as a visible participant, which feels awkward in sensitive client or medical calls.
3. Gemini notes save to Google Drive as a Google Doc with no central searchable library — notes are scattered and hard to find later.
4. Privacy concerns: users worry that Google processes audio through its cloud infrastructure, potentially using data for model training (confirmed concerns from Basil AI investigation, Feb 2026).
5. Language support gaps: Gemini only supports 8 languages vs. competitors that cover 90+ — a problem for multilingual teams.
6. No offline mode for desktop: Google's offline note-taking is currently only available on Android, not Mac or Windows desktop.

These should become H2 questions and FAQ entries.
