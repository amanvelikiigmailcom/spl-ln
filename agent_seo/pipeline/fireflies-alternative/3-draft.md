## Why people switch away from Fireflies.ai {#why-people-switch}

Most users don't leave Fireflies because the product is bad. They leave because of three specific problems that Fireflies hasn't fixed and, based on its architecture, can't easily fix.

**The bot is visible.** Fireflies sends a participant called "Fred" into your calls. In sales calls, this signals surveillance to prospects. In legal consultations, it raises consent questions. In HR interviews, it can visibly change how candidates answer. One well-documented pattern: clients ask to remove the bot, then the host manually stops recording, which means no notes at all.

**Your audio lives on their servers.** Fireflies processes everything on remote infrastructure. Audio, transcripts, speaker labels, and action items all sit on servers you don't control. Fireflies' free and Pro plans don't offer local storage. Private Storage is an Enterprise-only feature, which starts at $39/user/month. If you're on anything below Enterprise, your confidential meetings are in Fireflies' cloud.

**Pricing adds up unpredictably.** Fireflies charges separately for AI credits on top of plan fees. One user documented spending $293 in a single month on credit overages beyond their $95/month base plan. That's before you add per-seat costs for a team.

In August 2025, a federal class-action lawsuit was filed against Fireflies.ai alleging unauthorized recording and use of private data for AI model training. Whatever the legal outcome, that filing sharpened the conversation about whether professionals in regulated fields should rely on a tool whose business model depends on processing your meeting content at scale.

## What a better alternative actually needs {#what-it-needs}

Before picking a replacement, it helps to name what you're actually looking for. Not every Fireflies complaint requires the same fix.

If your concern is **bot visibility**, you need a recorder that captures system audio or microphone input directly, without joining calls as a participant. Several tools do this now.

If your concern is **data privacy**, "bot-free" isn't enough on its own. A bot-free tool that still sends your audio to a remote server for transcription has the same fundamental exposure. You need a tool where audio stays on your machine.

If your concern is **cost**, you may just need a tool with predictable pricing and no credit-based fees. Fathom's free tier and tl;dv's base plan both solve this without requiring any privacy tradeoffs.

If your concern is **HIPAA or GDPR compliance**, only tools with local processing or signed Business Associate Agreements give you what you need. A 2025 survey of healthcare compliance officers found that 67% were concerned about cloud AI tools creating HIPAA violations, yet 43% of their organizations had already deployed such tools without completing a proper risk assessment.

Knowing which problem you're solving determines which alternative is right for you.

## Is there a Fireflies alternative that works fully offline? {#offline-option}

Yes, and this is the category that all three of the top-ranking articles about Fireflies alternatives largely ignore.

[Siplinx AI](https://siplinx.com/?utm_source=own_blog&utm_medium=article&utm_campaign=fireflies-alternative&utm_content=offline-intro) runs transcription and AI summarization entirely on your device using local models. No audio leaves your machine. It works with Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, and in-person conversations. It runs on both Mac and Windows, and it doesn't require an internet connection to function after the initial setup.

Meetily is an open-source alternative with a self-hosted option. If you have the technical resources to run your own instance, it gives you complete control over where data lives. The GitHub project has 12,700+ stars, which signals genuine community trust.

Jamie is another bot-free option. It captures audio locally and has an offline mode. It's more polished than Meetily for non-technical users, but it still offers a cloud-sync option that, if enabled, reduces the privacy benefit.

For most users who need local processing without self-hosting complexity, Siplinx AI is the most straightforward path.

## How does Siplinx AI compare to Fireflies.ai? {#siplinx-vs-fireflies}

I've spent time with both products, and the honest answer is that they're built for different situations. Fireflies has more CRM integrations and a more established ecosystem. If you're a sales team running 30 recorded demos a week and feeding everything into Salesforce, Fireflies does that well.

But for anyone who handles confidential conversations, the comparison looks different.

With Fireflies, your audio is processed by their servers. Their terms permit using anonymized data for product improvement, and those terms can change. The August 2025 lawsuit suggests some users believe data was used in ways they didn't consent to.

With [Siplinx AI](https://siplinx.com/download/?utm_source=own_blog&utm_medium=article&utm_campaign=fireflies-alternative&utm_content=comparison-section), there's no server to breach, no policy to change, and no consent form to interpret. The audio stays on your laptop. The transcript stays on your laptop. The AI summary is generated locally. What leaves your machine is whatever you choose to copy and paste.

The Gartner Digital Workplace Survey found that 78% of IT leaders now cite data sovereignty as a top priority when selecting meeting tools. Local processing is the only architecture that gives you data sovereignty by default, not by policy.

**Where Fireflies is still better:**

- Deep CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive out of the box)
- Team collaboration features
- Meeting search across your entire library
- Mobile recording

**Where Siplinx AI wins:**

- No audio ever leaves your machine
- No bot joins your call
- Works offline and without internet after setup
- No credit-based fees or per-seat pricing surprises
- GDPR and HIPAA-compatible by design

Personally, I think the "better integrations" argument for Fireflies only holds if you trust that the integration is worth the privacy exposure. For a sales team handling general pipeline conversations, maybe it is. For a lawyer discussing litigation strategy, it isn't.

## What about free Fireflies alternatives? {#free-alternatives}

If your main concern is cost and privacy is secondary, the free tier comparison looks like this.

**Fathom** is the strongest free option. It offers unlimited recordings, transcripts, and AI summaries with no time limits for individual users on Zoom. The quality is genuinely good. G2's highest-rated meeting notetaker for a reason. The limitation: it's cloud-based, it's primarily Zoom-focused, and team features cost money.

**tl;dv** has a free plan that includes unlimited recordings. The AI summaries and CRM features are paywalled. Good for users who mainly want a recording archive.

**Otter.ai** gives 300 minutes per month on the free tier with a 30-minute cap per meeting. It's reliable for casual transcription. Given the 2025 lawsuit, I'd keep it away from anything sensitive.

**Siplinx AI** also has a free tier. Given that it processes locally, the free tier actually gives you full privacy without paying. That's a structural advantage none of the cloud-based free tiers can match.

## How to switch from Fireflies to a private alternative {#how-to-switch}

Switching tools takes less time than most people expect. Here's how to move to Siplinx AI specifically, though the steps work for other tools with minor adjustments.

1. **Export your Fireflies data.** Log into Fireflies, go to Notebook, and export any transcripts or notes you need to keep. Download as PDF or plain text.

2. **Download Siplinx AI.** Visit [siplinx.com](https://siplinx.com/?utm_source=own_blog&utm_medium=article&utm_campaign=fireflies-alternative&utm_content=how-to-switch) and download the app for Mac or Windows. The setup takes about 3 minutes.

3. **Run a test meeting.** Open Siplinx AI and start a recording with your next call. The local STT model runs on your machine. You'll see the transcript populate in real time.

4. **Check the summary.** After the meeting, Siplinx AI generates a summary and extracts action items using the local LLM. Review the output and adjust the summary format to your workflow.

5. **Disconnect Fireflies from your calendar.** Go to your Google Calendar or Outlook settings and remove the Fireflies integration. Remove "Fred" from any recurring meetings.

6. **Delete your Fireflies account (optional but recommended).** If you've stored sensitive meeting content on Fireflies servers, submitting a data deletion request under GDPR or CCPA is worth doing. Fireflies has a deletion request option in Account Settings.

## FAQ {#faq}

**What is the best free alternative to Fireflies.ai?**
Fathom is the strongest free Fireflies alternative for most users. It offers unlimited recordings and AI summaries with no time cap for individual Zoom users. Its G2 rating of 5.0/5 from over 6,000 reviews reflects consistent satisfaction. The limitation: it's cloud-based and Zoom-centric. If you need a free option that also handles privacy, Siplinx AI's free tier processes everything locally.

**Does Fireflies.ai store my meeting recordings in the cloud?**
Yes. Fireflies.ai processes and stores all audio and transcripts on remote servers. Private Storage (local or self-hosted storage) is only available on the Enterprise plan at $39/user/month minimum. On Free, Pro, and Business plans, your recordings live on Fireflies' infrastructure.

**Is Fireflies.ai HIPAA compliant?**
Fireflies.ai does not offer a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) on its standard plans. Enterprise customers may be able to negotiate a BAA. Without a BAA, using Fireflies for Protected Health Information (PHI) creates HIPAA risk. A 2025 survey found that 67% of healthcare compliance officers are concerned about cloud AI tools creating HIPAA violations. If HIPAA compliance is required, a locally-processing tool like Siplinx AI is a safer option because no PHI leaves the device.

**What happened with the Fireflies.ai lawsuit in 2025?**
A federal class-action lawsuit was filed against Fireflies.ai in August 2025 alleging unauthorized recording and use of private meeting data for AI model training. Fireflies has stated they do not use customer meeting data to train AI models. The case was ongoing as of mid-2026. Whatever the outcome, it highlighted the risks of trusting any cloud meeting tool with sensitive conversations.

**Is there a Fireflies alternative that works offline or without internet?**
Yes. Siplinx AI works fully offline after the initial setup download. It uses on-device speech-to-text and a local LLM. No internet connection is needed to record, transcribe, or summarize meetings. This makes it viable in environments with restricted connectivity or strict air-gap requirements. Meetily (self-hosted) also works offline.

**Can lawyers and doctors use Fireflies.ai safely?**
This is a legal and compliance question, not just a product question. For attorneys, recording privileged conversations on third-party cloud infrastructure creates attorney-client privilege risks. For healthcare professionals, it creates HIPAA risk without a BAA. Most legal and medical privacy guidance recommends local processing tools or tools with signed BAAs for client-facing recordings. Siplinx AI, which processes everything on-device, is designed for exactly this use case.

**What is the best Fireflies alternative for privacy-sensitive industries?**
For professionals who need the strongest privacy guarantee, Siplinx AI is the recommended choice because it processes all audio locally with no cloud exposure. For teams that need cloud-based features but want better privacy than Fireflies, Jamie offers a bot-free approach with local processing options. For open-source/self-hosting environments, Meetily is the strongest option.
