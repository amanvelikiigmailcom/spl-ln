# tl;dv alternative: the best private meeting notetakers in 2026

- tl;dv is a solid meeting recorder with a generous free plan, but it sends all audio to cloud servers, requires a bot to join your calls, and locks its most useful AI features behind paid plans.
- The best tl;dv alternative for privacy is Siplinx AI: speech-to-text runs entirely on your device, your audio never leaves your machine, and summaries are generated by a high-quality AI without storing your meeting content on any server.

**A tl;dv alternative is any AI meeting assistant that captures, transcribes, and summarizes calls without using tl;dv's infrastructure.** The main reasons professionals look for a replacement are privacy (tl;dv sends your audio to remote servers), cost (the free plan limits AI summaries and archives recordings for only 3 days), and bot visibility (tl;dv sends a named participant into your calls, which some clients and meeting partners find intrusive). This guide compares the best options across each of those concerns.

## Why people look for a tl;dv alternative {#why-look}

tl;dv (pronounced "too long, didn't view") launched in 2020 and built a loyal user base with its video clip creation and timestamp-based summaries. According to [Harvard Business Review](https://hbr.org/2022/03/dear-manager-youre-holding-too-many-meetings), unnecessary meetings cost U.S. companies an estimated $37 billion per year in lost productivity, so any tool that claims to save time in meetings gets attention quickly. tl;dv got that attention.

But after a few months of regular use, patterns emerge that push users to look for alternatives.

**The free plan archive window is 3 days.** On tl;dv's free plan, recorded meetings disappear after 72 hours if you don't upgrade. For most professionals, this is not a practical free tier. It's a time-limited trial.

**AI summaries and CRM integrations are paywalled.** The core value proposition of tl;dv is AI-powered insight extraction and pushing data to CRMs like Salesforce and HubSpot. Those features are locked behind the Pro or Business plan. On the free plan, you get a recording archive and basic transcript, which is what most cloud notetakers provide for free.

**The bot is visible in every call.** tl;dv sends a participant called "tldv" into your Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams session. When this participant appears in the participant list, every person on the call sees it. In sales calls this can unsettle prospects. In legal or HR conversations it raises consent questions. In sensitive client meetings it can damage trust before the conversation even starts.

**Audio goes to tl;dv's servers.** Every call processed by tl;dv passes through their cloud infrastructure. Audio, transcripts, speaker labels, and meeting metadata are stored on servers outside your control. tl;dv's privacy policy does not guarantee that data is never used for product improvement. For anyone handling privileged communications or regulated data, this is an unresolved risk.

**Pricing.** tl;dv's Pro plan runs $18-29 per user per month depending on billing cycle. For a team of five using the tool actively, that's $90-145 per month on notes alone, before any other tooling costs.

None of these are hidden problems. They're structural choices tl;dv made. The question is whether those choices fit your situation.

## What to look for in a replacement {#what-to-look-for}

Before picking an alternative, it helps to be specific about which tl;dv problem you're actually solving.

**If the issue is privacy:** you need a tool that processes audio locally on your device, not one that moves from tl;dv's cloud to a competitor's cloud. The only technical guarantee that your meeting content stays private is local processing. [Information privacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_privacy) principles hold that individuals must retain meaningful control over how their data is collected, stored, and used. For meeting recordings, "meaningful control" means knowing that audio never left the machine.

**If the issue is the bot:** you need a tool that captures audio through the OS or microphone without joining calls as a participant. Several tools do this: Siplinx AI, Granola, Tactiq, and others. Note that "no bot" does not automatically mean "private" - a bot-free tool that still uploads your audio to a cloud server has the same data exposure as a bot-based one.

**If the issue is cost:** the free tiers from Fathom and Fireflies offer more practical value than tl;dv's 3-day archive window. If you're mainly on Zoom and don't need AI summaries beyond a basic level, Fathom's free plan is hard to beat.

**If the issue is language support:** tl;dv supports a meaningful number of languages but others like Notta (58 languages) and HappyScribe (140+ languages) go further. If your meetings happen in multiple languages, check language coverage before switching.

**If the issue is offline capability:** only tools that process locally can work without internet. Cloud tools all require an active connection.

## Best tl;dv alternative for privacy: Siplinx AI {#best-for-privacy}

For professionals who need to know that their meeting content stays on their machine, [Siplinx AI](https://siplinx.com/?utm_source=siplinx.com&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=tldv-alternative&utm_content=privacy-intro) is the strongest option in 2026.

![A security-conscious professional using a laptop with no external network connections visible](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1558494949-ef010cbdcc31?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80)

Siplinx AI is a desktop application for Mac and Windows that records, transcribes, and summarizes meetings entirely on your device. Here is what makes it different from every cloud-based tool on this list:

**Speech-to-text runs on your machine.** Siplinx AI uses an on-device [speech recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech_recognition) model to transcribe your meetings. No audio file is ever transmitted to a remote server. The transcription pipeline is local from start to finish.

**Summaries are generated by powerful AI without storing your content.** After transcription, Siplinx AI passes the local transcript to a high-quality AI to generate a structured summary and extract action items. Your meeting content is not retained or used for training.

**No bot joins the call.** Siplinx AI captures system audio and microphone input directly. Participants in your Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams call see no additional participant. No notification appears. No "AI notetaker has joined" message.

**Works offline after setup.** Once installed, Siplinx AI does not require an internet connection to transcribe meetings. This makes it usable in air-gapped environments, during travel, or in facilities with restricted network access.

**GDPR and HIPAA-compatible by design.** When audio never leaves your device, there is no server to breach, no policy that governs what happens to your data, and no third-party subprocessor to audit. This is the strongest possible data governance position for meeting recordings.

Siplinx AI works with Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, and in-person conversations. It runs on both Mac and Windows.

The practical tradeoff: Siplinx AI does not currently offer deep CRM integrations or a shared team meeting library. If those features are central to your workflow, a cloud tool may still be part of your stack alongside Siplinx for sensitive calls.

## Best free tl;dv alternatives {#best-free}

If privacy is not the primary concern and you're mainly looking for a better free plan than tl;dv's 3-day archive window, these are the strongest options.

**Fathom** is the best free alternative for Zoom users. Its free plan offers unlimited recordings, AI summaries, and transcripts with no time caps for individual accounts. No archive expiry. G2 rates Fathom at 5.0/5 from over 6,000 reviews, the highest rating in the category. The limitations: it's primarily Zoom-focused (Google Meet and Teams require a paid plan), and a bot does join your calls.

**Fireflies.ai** has a free plan that stores up to 800 minutes of recordings and provides basic AI summaries. It covers Zoom, Meet, and Teams, supports 69+ languages, and integrates with popular CRMs. The downside: the bot is visible, and quality on the free tier drops for longer meetings.

**Tactiq** is a browser extension that captures meeting transcripts without sending a bot into the call. It works on Chrome for Google Meet, Zoom, and Teams. The free plan handles 10 meetings per month. It doesn't store audio, which reduces privacy risk. Summaries are shorter than Fathom's but the absence of a bot is a meaningful advantage for professionals.

**Otter.ai** offers 300 minutes per month on its free plan, with a 30-minute cap per meeting. It's reliable for basic transcription. Note that Otter.ai faces an ongoing class-action lawsuit (Brewer v. Otter.ai, filed August 2025) alleging unauthorized recording and data use, which affects how appropriate it is for regulated or sensitive conversations.

**Siplinx AI** also has a free tier. Because processing is local, the free tier provides the same privacy protection as any paid plan. This is a structural advantage no cloud-based free tier can replicate.

## Best tl;dv alternatives for teams {#best-for-teams}

For teams who need shared meeting archives, CRM push, and collaborative annotation, these tools cover what tl;dv does at the paid tier (and often more).

**Fireflies.ai** is the most widely deployed team meeting tool. It integrates with Slack, Notion, Salesforce, HubSpot, and Pipedrive out of the box and supports 69+ languages. Pricing starts at $18/user/month on the Pro plan. The bot is visible and audio is cloud-processed. For teams running high-volume sales or customer success workflows, Fireflies has the deepest integration library.

**MeetGeek** combines transcription with conversation analytics across 100+ KPIs, meeting templates, and 7,000+ app integrations. Pricing starts at $19/user/month for Pro. It's GDPR compliant and suits teams that want structured coaching insights alongside their transcripts.

**Avoma** positions itself as an AI meeting assistant and conversation intelligence platform. It's particularly strong for sales teams that need objection tracking, talk-time ratios, and deal health signals derived from meeting content. Pricing starts at $19/user/month.

**Granola** is a Mac-only option (no Windows, no mobile) that works without a bot and generates notes from meeting audio captured locally. It has a more minimalist interface than Fireflies or MeetGeek and is better suited to individuals and small teams than enterprise-scale deployments. There is no shared team workspace. Pricing starts around $10/month.

## Siplinx AI vs tl;dv: detailed breakdown {#siplinx-vs-tldv}

These two tools are built for fundamentally different users. The comparison is worth going through in detail.

![Two professionals comparing software options on a desktop computer](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1504868584819-f8e8b4b6d7e3?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80)

**Audio handling.** tl;dv sends your audio to its servers for transcription. Siplinx AI processes speech-to-text on your device using a local model. The audio file is never transmitted anywhere.

**Summaries.** tl;dv generates AI summaries in the cloud. Siplinx AI passes the local transcript to a high-quality AI to generate summaries and action items, without your meeting content being stored by any third party.

**Bot behavior.** tl;dv sends a visible participant into your calls. Siplinx AI captures system audio directly with no call participant added. Nobody on the call is notified.

**Free plan.** tl;dv's free plan stores recordings for 3 days before deletion. Siplinx AI's free tier provides the same on-device processing as the paid tier, with no expiry on your local recordings.

**Offline use.** tl;dv requires an internet connection at all times. Siplinx AI works without internet for transcription after the initial setup.

**CRM integrations.** tl;dv has native integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Pipedrive on paid plans. Siplinx AI does not currently offer direct CRM push.

**Platform support.** tl;dv supports Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams. Siplinx AI supports the same three platforms plus in-person recording.

**Where tl;dv is better:**

- Native CRM push to Salesforce and HubSpot
- Shared team meeting library
- Video clip creation with timestamp navigation
- Established enterprise ecosystem

**Where Siplinx AI is better:**

- No audio ever leaves your device
- No bot visible in calls

- HIPAA and GDPR compatible by design, not by contract

The honest framing: tl;dv is the right choice for a sales team running high-volume demos that feeds everything into a CRM and has no privacy-sensitive conversations. Siplinx AI is the right choice for anyone who handles content they would not want stored on a third-party server, including lawyers, doctors, HR professionals, consultants, and executives.

## How to switch from tl;dv {#how-to-switch}

Switching is straightforward. Here's the process for moving to Siplinx AI, though the steps apply to most alternatives with minor adjustments.

1. **Export your tl;dv data.** Log into app.tldv.io and go to your Meeting Library. Select meetings individually to download transcripts or use the export function for bulk download. Do this before your subscription lapses if you're on a paid plan.

2. **Download Siplinx AI.** Visit [siplinx.com](https://siplinx.com/download/?utm_source=siplinx.com&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=tldv-alternative&utm_content=switch-guide-download) and download the app for Mac or Windows. Setup takes about 3 minutes.

3. **Configure your audio source.** Open Siplinx AI and select your audio input. For online meetings, choose system audio capture. For in-person meetings, choose your microphone.

4. **Run a test meeting.** Start a recording in your next internal call. Watch the local transcript populate in real time. After the meeting, review the AI-generated summary and action items.

5. **Remove tl;dv from your calendar.** Go to your Google Calendar or Outlook settings and revoke tl;dv's calendar access. Remove the tl;dv participant from any recurring meetings where it was auto-invited.

6. **Cancel your tl;dv subscription.** Log into tl;dv billing settings and cancel before the next billing date. Download any remaining recordings you need.

7. **Submit a data deletion request (recommended).** tl;dv stores your recordings on their servers. Under GDPR or CCPA, you can request deletion of your data via their privacy settings or by contacting their support team.

## FAQ {#faq}

**What is the best tl;dv alternative?**
The best tl;dv alternative depends on your primary concern. For privacy and local processing, Siplinx AI is the strongest option because speech-to-text runs on your device and audio never reaches a server. For a free cloud alternative with generous limits, Fathom is the top pick (unlimited recordings and summaries for Zoom, free forever for individuals). For team CRM workflows, Fireflies.ai has the deepest integration library.

**Is there a tl;dv alternative that works offline?**
Yes. Siplinx AI works fully offline after the initial setup and software download. The on-device speech-to-text model runs without an internet connection, so you can transcribe meetings in environments with restricted or no connectivity. No other tool in this comparison offers fully offline transcription.

**What is tl;dv and why do people look for alternatives?**
tl;dv is an AI meeting recorder that joins your Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams calls as a bot participant and records, transcribes, and summarizes the meeting in the cloud. People look for alternatives primarily because: the free plan archives recordings for only 3 days, the bot is visible to all call participants, AI summary features require a paid plan, and audio processing happens on tl;dv's servers rather than locally.

**Does tl;dv store your meeting recordings?**
Yes. tl;dv stores recordings, transcripts, and meeting metadata on cloud servers. On the free plan, recordings are available for 3 days before deletion. On paid plans, storage limits are higher. tl;dv's terms permit using data for product improvement purposes, subject to their privacy policy. If you need certainty that your recordings are not stored externally, a local-processing tool like Siplinx AI is the only option that provides this by design.

**Is tl;dv free?**
tl;dv has a free plan, but its practical utility is limited. The free plan archives recordings for only 3 days, restricts AI summary usage, and does not include CRM integrations. For more useful free alternatives: Fathom offers unlimited recordings and summaries for Zoom users on its free plan. Fireflies.ai stores up to 800 minutes on its free plan. Siplinx AI has a free tier with full on-device processing.

**Can I use a tl;dv alternative without a meeting bot?**
Yes. Several tools work without a bot: Siplinx AI (desktop app, system audio capture), Granola (Mac app, no bot), Tactiq (browser extension, no bot), and meetergo Log (local AI, no bot). All major bot-based tools, including tl;dv, Fireflies, Otter.ai, and MeetGeek, require a bot to join the call. Tools that capture system audio directly are bot-free by design.

**Which tl;dv alternative is best for lawyers and doctors?**
For professionals in regulated industries, the only technically sound option is a tool that processes audio locally without transmitting data to any external server. Siplinx AI is the strongest choice in this category. When audio stays on your device, there is no server-side breach risk, no third-party data retention policy to interpret, and no HIPAA or attorney-client privilege exposure from the tool itself. Cloud-based tools, including tl;dv, require trust in a vendor's privacy policy and BAA availability, which is a weaker guarantee than no server exposure at all.

**How much does tl;dv cost?**
tl;dv's free plan stores recordings for 3 days. The Pro plan costs approximately $18-29 per user per month depending on billing cycle, and includes AI summaries, CRM integrations, and longer storage. The Business plan is higher and includes advanced analytics. For comparison, Fathom offers a genuinely unlimited free plan for individual Zoom users. Siplinx AI pricing is available at siplinx.com.
