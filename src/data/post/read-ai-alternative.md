This guide covers the best options across each of those concerns.

## Why people look for a Read AI alternative {#why-look}

Read AI launched in 2021 with a feature that set it apart from basic transcription tools: Meeting Scores. After each call, Read AI assigns a numerical score to the meeting based on behavioral signals such as talk time distribution, sentiment patterns, and engagement metrics derived from participant behavior. The idea is to give teams data they can act on, not just a transcript.

In practice, that feature surfaces the central objection many professionals have with Read AI: it is not just capturing what people said. It is measuring how people behaved.

According to [Harvard Business Review](https://hbr.org/2022/03/dear-manager-youre-holding-too-many-meetings), unnecessary meetings cost U.S. companies an estimated $37 billion per year in lost productivity, which explains why meeting intelligence tools gain traction quickly. But the same professional pressure that makes these tools attractive also makes their data practices worth scrutinizing.

Here are the specific reasons users leave Read AI.

**The bot announces itself.** Read AI joins calls as a visible participant. Everyone on the call sees it. In a sales call this can make prospects uncomfortable. In a legal or HR context it creates consent questions that are difficult to resolve on the spot. In sensitive client meetings it changes how participants speak before the first agenda item comes up.

**All audio is processed in the cloud.** Read AI sends your meeting recordings to remote servers for transcription, summarization, and behavioral analysis. Audio, transcripts, speaker identification data, and engagement metrics all pass through infrastructure outside your control. Read AI's privacy policy does not rule out using this data to improve its AI models. For anyone handling legally privileged communications, patient conversations, or regulated data, that is an unresolved liability.

**The Meeting Score creates behavioral records that participants did not consent to.** Most participants know they are being recorded. Fewer expect that an AI will score their sentiment and engagement level and present that data to the meeting host. This dimension of Read AI's product has drawn criticism from HR professionals and legal teams who point out that behavioral scoring of employees or clients may have implications beyond standard recording consent.

**The free plan is functionally limited.** Read AI's free tier allows 5 meetings per month with a 30-minute cap per meeting. For any regular professional use, this is not workable without upgrading.

**Pricing.** Read AI's Pro plan runs $19.75 per user per month. For a team of five, that is nearly $100 per month on meeting notes before any other tooling costs.

None of these are hidden problems. They are design choices Read AI made. The question is whether those choices fit your situation.

## What to look for in a replacement {#what-to-look-for}

Before picking an alternative, it helps to be specific about which Read AI problem you are actually solving.

**If the issue is privacy:** you need a tool that processes audio locally on your device, not one that simply moves from Read AI's cloud to a competitor's cloud. The only technical guarantee that your meeting content stays private is local processing. [Information privacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_privacy) principles hold that individuals and organizations must retain meaningful control over how their data is collected, stored, and used. For meeting recordings, "meaningful control" means knowing that audio never left the machine.

**If the issue is the bot:** you need a tool that captures audio through the operating system or microphone without joining calls as a participant. Several tools do this: Siplinx AI, Granola, Tactiq, and others. Note that "no bot" does not automatically mean "private." A bot-free tool that still sends your audio to a cloud server has the same data exposure as a bot-based one.

**If the issue is behavioral scoring:** you need a tool that generates summaries and action items without also measuring and storing participant engagement data. Most basic meeting recorders do not do behavioral analysis. Siplinx AI, Granola, Fathom, and others focus on transcription and summaries without the scoring layer.

**If the issue is cost:** the free tiers from Fathom and Fireflies offer more practical value than Read AI's 5-meeting cap. If you are mainly on Zoom and do not need behavioral analytics, Fathom's free plan is hard to beat.

**If the issue is offline capability:** only tools that process locally can work without internet. Cloud tools all require an active connection.

## Best Read AI alternative for privacy: Siplinx AI {#best-for-privacy}

For professionals who need to know that their meeting content stays on their machine, [Siplinx AI](https://siplinx.com/?utm_source=siplinx.com&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=read-ai-alternative&utm_content=privacy-intro) is the strongest option in 2026.

![A security-conscious professional using a laptop with no external network connections visible](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1558494949-ef010cbdcc31?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80)

Siplinx AI is a desktop application for Mac and Windows that records, transcribes, and summarizes meetings entirely on your device. Here is what makes it different from every cloud-based tool on this list.

**Speech-to-text runs on your machine.** Siplinx AI uses an on-device [speech recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech_recognition) model to transcribe your meetings. No audio file is ever transmitted to a remote server. The transcription pipeline is local from start to finish.

**Summaries are generated by a high-quality AI without storing your content.** After transcription, Siplinx AI passes the local transcript to a powerful AI to generate a structured summary and extract action items. Your meeting content is not retained or used for training.

**No bot joins the call.** Siplinx AI captures system audio and microphone input directly. Participants in your Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams call see no additional participant. No notification appears. No behavioral scoring happens.

**Works offline after setup.** Once installed, Siplinx AI does not require an internet connection to transcribe meetings. This makes it usable in air-gapped environments, during travel, or in facilities with restricted network access.

**GDPR and HIPAA-compatible by design.** When audio never leaves your device, there is no server to breach, no policy that governs what happens to your data, and no third-party subprocessor to audit. This is the strongest possible data governance position for meeting recordings.

**No behavioral scoring.** Siplinx AI does not measure your participants' sentiment or engagement. It captures what was said and produces a clean summary. What your participants do with their faces and voices during a meeting is not recorded, analyzed, or stored.

Siplinx AI works with Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, and in-person conversations. It runs on both Mac and Windows.

The practical tradeoff: Siplinx AI does not currently offer deep CRM integrations or a shared team meeting library. If those features are central to your workflow, a cloud tool may be part of your stack for non-sensitive calls, with Siplinx for anything confidential.

## Best free Read AI alternatives {#best-free}

If privacy is not the primary concern and you are mainly looking for a better free plan than Read AI's 5-meeting cap, these are the strongest options.

**Fathom** is the best free alternative for Zoom users. Its free plan offers unlimited recordings, AI summaries, and transcripts with no time caps for individual accounts. G2 rates Fathom at 5.0/5 from over 6,000 reviews, the highest rating in this category. The limitations: it is primarily Zoom-focused (Google Meet and Teams require a paid plan), and a bot does join your calls. No behavioral scoring.

**Fireflies.ai** has a free plan that stores up to 800 minutes of recordings and provides basic AI summaries. It covers Zoom, Meet, and Teams, supports 69+ languages, and integrates with popular CRMs. The downside: the bot is visible, and quality on the free tier drops for longer meetings.

**Tactiq** is a browser extension that captures meeting transcripts without sending a bot into the call. It works on Chrome for Google Meet, Zoom, and Teams. The free plan handles 10 meetings per month. It does not store audio, which reduces privacy risk. Summaries are shorter than Fathom's but the absence of a bot is a meaningful advantage for professionals.

**Otter.ai** offers 300 minutes per month on its free plan, with a 30-minute cap per meeting. It is reliable for basic transcription. Note that Otter.ai faces an ongoing class-action lawsuit (Brewer v. Otter.ai, filed August 2025) alleging unauthorized recording and data use, which affects how appropriate it is for regulated or sensitive conversations.

**Siplinx AI** also has a free tier. Because processing is local, the free tier provides the same privacy protection as any paid plan. This is a structural advantage no cloud-based free tier can replicate.

## Best Read AI alternatives for teams {#best-for-teams}

For teams who need shared meeting archives, CRM push, and collaborative annotation, these tools cover what Read AI does at the paid tier (and in some cases more).

**Fireflies.ai** is the most widely deployed team meeting tool. It integrates with Slack, Notion, Salesforce, HubSpot, and Pipedrive out of the box and supports 69+ languages. Pricing starts at $18 per user per month on the Pro plan. The bot is visible and audio is cloud-processed. For teams running high-volume sales or customer success workflows, Fireflies has the deepest integration library.

**MeetGeek** combines transcription with conversation analytics across 100+ KPIs, meeting templates, and 7,000+ app integrations. Pricing starts at $19 per user per month for Pro. It is GDPR compliant and suits teams that want structured coaching insights alongside their transcripts. Unlike Read AI, MeetGeek does not publish Meeting Scores, so the behavioral analytics layer is less prominent.

**Avoma** positions itself as an AI meeting assistant and conversation intelligence platform. It is particularly strong for sales teams that need objection tracking, talk-time ratios, and deal health signals. Pricing starts at $19 per user per month. It operates in the cloud.

**Granola** is a Mac-only option that works without a bot and generates notes from meeting audio captured locally on your computer, then processed in the cloud for AI summaries. It has a cleaner interface than Fireflies or MeetGeek and is better suited to individuals and small teams. There is no shared team workspace. Pricing starts around $10 per month.

## Siplinx AI vs Read AI: detailed breakdown {#siplinx-vs-read}

These two tools are built for fundamentally different users. The comparison is worth going through in detail.

![Two professionals comparing software options on a desktop computer](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1504868584819-f8e8b4b6d7e3?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80)

**Audio handling.** Read AI sends your audio to its servers for transcription and analysis. Siplinx AI processes speech-to-text on your device using a local model. The audio file is never transmitted anywhere.

**Summaries.** Read AI generates AI summaries in the cloud as part of its full analytics pipeline. Siplinx AI passes the local transcript to a high-quality AI to generate summaries and action items, without your meeting content being stored by any third party.

**Bot behavior.** Read AI sends a visible participant into your calls. Siplinx AI captures system audio directly with no call participant added. Nobody on the call is notified.

**Behavioral analytics.** Read AI assigns Meeting Scores and tracks participant engagement metrics. Siplinx AI produces a clean transcript and summary. It does not measure or score participant behavior.

**Free plan.** Read AI's free plan covers 5 meetings per month. Siplinx AI's free tier provides the same on-device processing as the paid tier.

**Offline use.** Read AI requires an internet connection at all times. Siplinx AI works without internet for transcription after the initial setup.

**Platform support.** Read AI supports Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, and Webex. Siplinx AI supports Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, and in-person recording.

**Where Read AI is better:**

- Meeting Scores and engagement analytics for teams that want behavioral data
- Native Webex support
- Shared team meeting library with searchable archive
- Video playback with transcript highlights
- Real-time summaries during the call

**Where Siplinx AI is better:**

- No audio ever leaves your device
- No bot visible in calls
- No behavioral scoring of participants
- Fully offline after initial setup
- No risk of your meeting content being stored externally
- HIPAA and GDPR compatible by design, not by contract
- No 5-meeting cap on the free tier

The honest framing: Read AI is the right choice for a team that wants behavioral meeting analytics, is comfortable with cloud processing, and does not handle privacy-sensitive conversations. Siplinx AI is the right choice for anyone who handles content they would not want stored on a third-party server and who does not need their participants' behavior scored.

A Gartner Digital Workplace Survey found that 78% of IT leaders now cite data sovereignty as a top priority when selecting meeting tools. Local processing is the only architecture that provides data sovereignty by design rather than by policy.

## How to switch from Read AI {#how-to-switch}

Switching is straightforward. Here is the process for moving to Siplinx AI, though the general steps apply to most alternatives.

1. **Export your Read AI data.** Log into read.ai and go to your Meeting History. Download transcripts and summaries from meetings you want to keep before canceling. Read AI allows transcript export in PDF and text formats from individual meeting pages.

2. **Download Siplinx AI.** Visit [siplinx.com](https://siplinx.com/download/?utm_source=siplinx.com&utm_medium=blog&utm_campaign=read-ai-alternative&utm_content=switch-guide-download) and download the app for Mac or Windows. Setup takes about 3 minutes.

3. **Configure your audio source.** Open Siplinx AI and select your audio input. For online meetings, choose system audio capture. For in-person meetings, choose your microphone.

4. **Run a test meeting.** Start a recording in your next internal call. Watch the local transcript populate in real time. After the meeting, review the AI-generated summary and action items.

5. **Remove Read AI from your calendar.** Go to your Google Calendar or Outlook settings and revoke Read AI's calendar access. Remove the Read AI participant from any recurring meetings where it was auto-invited.

6. **Cancel your Read AI subscription.** Log into Read AI billing settings and cancel before the next billing date. Download any remaining transcripts you need.

7. **Submit a data deletion request (recommended).** Read AI stores your recordings on their servers. Under GDPR or CCPA, you can request deletion of your data via their privacy settings or by contacting their support team directly.

## FAQ {#faq}

**What is the best Read AI alternative?**

The best Read AI alternative depends on your primary concern. For privacy and local processing, Siplinx AI is the strongest option because speech-to-text runs on your device and audio never reaches a server. For a free cloud alternative with generous limits, Fathom is the top pick for Zoom users (unlimited recordings and summaries, free forever for individuals). For team CRM workflows, Fireflies.ai has the deepest integration library. For a no-bot option on Mac, Granola is worth considering.

**Is there a Read AI alternative that works offline?**

Yes. Siplinx AI works fully offline after the initial setup and software download. The on-device speech-to-text model runs without an internet connection, so you can transcribe meetings in environments with restricted or no connectivity. No other tool in this comparison offers fully offline transcription.

**What is Read AI and why do people look for alternatives?**

Read AI is an AI meeting intelligence platform that joins your Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, or Webex calls as a bot participant and records, transcribes, and analyzes the meeting in the cloud. It is known for its "Meeting Scores" feature, which rates calls based on behavioral signals like sentiment and engagement. People look for alternatives primarily because: the free plan is limited to 5 meetings per month, the bot is visible to all call participants, all audio is processed on Read AI's servers, and the behavioral scoring creates data records that participants may not be aware of.

**Does Read AI store your meeting recordings?**

Yes. Read AI stores recordings, transcripts, summaries, and behavioral analytics on cloud servers. Read AI's terms allow using data for product improvement purposes. If you need certainty that your recordings are not stored externally, a local-processing tool like Siplinx AI is the only option that provides this by design.

**Is Read AI free?**

Read AI has a free plan, but it is limited to 5 meetings per month with a 30-minute cap per meeting. For regular professional use, this is not practical. For more useful free alternatives: Fathom offers unlimited recordings and summaries for Zoom users on its free plan. Fireflies.ai stores up to 800 minutes on its free plan. Siplinx AI has a free tier with full on-device processing.

**Can I use a Read AI alternative without a meeting bot?**

Yes. Several tools work without a bot: Siplinx AI (desktop app, system audio capture), Granola (Mac app, no bot), Tactiq (browser extension, no bot), and meetergo Log (local AI, no bot). All major bot-based tools, including Read AI, Fireflies, Otter.ai, MeetGeek, and tl;dv, require a bot to join the call. Tools that capture system audio directly are bot-free by design.

**Which Read AI alternative is best for lawyers and doctors?**

For professionals in regulated industries, the only technically sound option is a tool that processes audio locally without transmitting data to any external server. Siplinx AI is the strongest choice in this category. When audio stays on your device, there is no server-side breach risk, no third-party data retention policy to interpret, and no HIPAA or attorney-client privilege exposure from the tool itself. Cloud-based tools, including Read AI, require trust in a vendor's privacy policy and BAA availability, which is a weaker guarantee than no server exposure at all.

**Does Read AI score meeting participants without their consent?**

Read AI's Meeting Score feature analyzes behavioral signals including sentiment, talk time, and engagement metrics. Whether participants are adequately informed about this analysis depends on how the meeting host sets up the tool and what the host discloses to participants. In most cases, participants who see a "Read AI has joined" notification know they are being recorded, but may not know their engagement behavior is being scored. Legal and HR teams have raised concerns about this distinction, particularly for employee one-on-ones and client consultations.
